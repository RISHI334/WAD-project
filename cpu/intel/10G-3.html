Description

The Intel Core i3-10105 is a central processing unit (CPU) developed by Intel and released in 2020. It is part of the 10th Generation Intel Core processor family and is based on Intel's 14nm Comet Lake architecture.

The i3-10105 has four cores and eight threads, with a base clock speed of 3.7 GHz and a maximum turbo frequency of 4.4 GHz. It has 6 MB of Intel Smart Cache and a thermal design power (TDP) of 65 watts.

The i3-10105 supports dual-channel DDR4 memory with a maximum speed of 2666 MHz and has Intel UHD Graphics 630 with a maximum dynamic frequency of 1.05 GHz.

The i3-10105 is compatible with Intel's LGA 1200 socket and is designed for use in desktop systems that require a low-cost, entry-level CPU. It supports Intel Virtualization Technology (VT-x) and Intel Hyper-Threading Technology for improved performance in multi-tasking and virtualization scenarios.

Overall, the i3-10105 is a capable processor for applications such as office productivity, web browsing, and light multimedia tasks. Its low cost and power consumption make it a good choice for budget desktop systems that do not require high-end performance.
